Understanding Net Present Value (NPV)

Net Present Value is a key metric for evaluating investment opportunities:

$$ \text{NPV} = \sum_{t=0}^{n} \frac{C_t}{(1+r)^t} - C_0 $$

Where:

  • $C_t$ = Net cash inflow during period $t$
  • $C_0$ = Initial investment at time 0
  • $r$ = Discount rate (or rate of return that could be earned on an investment of comparable risk)
  • $t$ = Number of time periods
Explain that NPV helps us understand the current value of future cash flows, allowing us to make informed decisions about long-term investments. A positive NPV generally indicates a profitable project.
